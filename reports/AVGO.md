### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Exceptional (5/5)**

Broadcom's moat is the widest in AI infrastructure:
- **Tomahawk monopoly**: >80% share in high-speed Ethernet switching ASICs. Every AI data center uses Tomahawk. Without Broadcom switching silicon, the AI networking stack doesn't function.
- **Custom XPU dominance**: Designing custom AI chips for Google (TPU), Meta, and OpenAI. Multi-year $100B+ revenue opportunity. Each XPU program creates a 5-7 year revenue stream.
- **Optical DSP (Taurus)**: First 400G/lane DSP, competing with Marvell Ara and Credo Bluebird. Bundling switching + optical DSP creates a complete solution.
- **In-house EML + SiPh**: Broadcom makes its own EML lasers, photodiodes, AND silicon photonics transceivers -- vertically integrated.
- **CPO integration**: Co-packaging optics directly into switching ASICs and XPUs -- the ultimate convergence of compute and photonics.
- **68% EBITDA margin**: Software-like margins on hardware products.

### Supply Chain Position
Broadcom sits at the CENTER of the photonics supply chain:
- **Upstream**: TSMC (foundry), Tower Semiconductor (SiPh), Soitec (SOI wafers), ASE (packaging)
- **Downstream**: Arista (ANET) integrates Tomahawk in switches; Innolight/Coherent/AAOI use Broadcom components
- **Competes with**: Marvell (MRVL) in optical DSP + custom XPU; Credo (CRDO) in optical DSP; Intel in custom silicon

### Verdict: BUY
Broadcom is the most important company in AI infrastructure after NVIDIA -- it makes the switching silicon that every GPU cluster requires, the optical DSPs that every transceiver uses, and the custom AI chips that Google/Meta/OpenAI run on. At $323 with analyst target $431 (+39% upside), and AI revenue doubling, the risk/reward is attractive even at $1.53T market cap. The 68% EBITDA margin and $162B backlog provide extraordinary visibility.
