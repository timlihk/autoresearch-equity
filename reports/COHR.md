### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Coherent is the dominant pure-play in optical transceivers for AI data centers, uniquely offering three 1.6T architectures (silicon photonics, EML, VCSEL) and cementing its position via a $2B NVIDIA strategic partnership. The business is inflecting strongly on AI-driven demand with bookings extending to 2028, but the stock's ~50x forward P/E and negative trailing FCF price in much of the upside already. A great business at a full valuation.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Strong (4/5)**

Coherent has assembled a rare vertical integration stack in photonics:
- **Technology breadth**: Only company demonstrating all three 1.6T transceiver architectures at OFC 2026. This matters because hyperscalers (MSFT, GOOG, META, AMZN) each prefer different optical technologies.
- **NVIDIA partnership**: $2B strategic investment creates a deep lock-in with the dominant AI GPU platform. Coherent is the "optical backbone of AI."
- **Scale**: $6.3B revenue dwarfs most photonics competitors. The II-VI merger created a vertically integrated platform from materials to modules.
- **Switching costs**: Qualifying optical transceivers takes 12-18 months. Once designed in, customers rarely switch mid-generation.
- **Risk to moat**: Chinese competitors (Innolight/300308.SZ) are scaling fast on price, particularly in 800G. Pricing pressure is a real and ongoing threat.

### Catalysts
1. **1.6T transceiver ramp (H2 2026-2027)**: Next-gen data rate cycle is a major revenue driver; Coherent has the broadest product portfolio.
2. **NVIDIA partnership deepening**: Co-development of optical solutions for next-gen GPU clusters (Rubin/Blackwell Ultra).
3. **Margin expansion**: Non-GAAP gross margins trending from 37.9% to 39%+ as product mix shifts to higher-value datacenter optics.
4. **Bookings visibility to 2028**: Management noted multi-year commitments from hyperscalers, providing rare revenue visibility.
5. **Potential SiC/materials business IPO or spin-off**: Could unlock sum-of-parts value.

### Risks
1. **Valuation overshoot**: Stock up 273% in 52 weeks. Avg analyst target ($214) is BELOW current price, suggesting the market has run ahead of consensus.
2. **Negative trailing FCF**: Heavy capex spending on 1.6T capacity. If the ramp disappoints, FCF recovery could stall.
3. **Chinese competition**: Innolight is gaining share aggressively on 800G with lower pricing. Coherent must stay ahead on 1.6T to maintain margins.
4. **Customer concentration**: Hyperscaler capex is cyclical. Any slowdown in AI spending would hit Coherent disproportionately.
5. **Debt load**: $3.5B in long-term debt (legacy of II-VI merger). Paid down $400M in Q1 FY26 but still elevated.
6. **Execution risk on 1.6T yields**: If yield improvements stall, competitors could capture the ramp window.

### Valuation
At ~50x forward non-GAAP P/E and ~57x EV/EBITDA, Coherent is priced for a best-case 1.6T ramp scenario. The stock trades well above the average analyst target of $214. Even assuming non-GAAP EPS reaches $7-8 in FY27 (aggressive but plausible), that implies a forward P/E of 32-37x -- still premium but more digestible. The NVIDIA partnership and 1.6T leadership justify some premium, but at current levels, much of the AI optionality is already baked in. PEG ratio is unfavorable given GAAP profitability remains thin.

### Verdict: HOLD
Coherent is the right horse in the right race -- the optical interconnect bottleneck is real and Coherent has the best product portfolio to address it. However, the stock has re-rated dramatically and trades above consensus targets. Better entry points likely exist on any pullback tied to AI capex concerns or quarterly volatility. Initiate positions on dips below $200.
