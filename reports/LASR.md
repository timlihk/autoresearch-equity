# LASR - nLIGHT Inc
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 49/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
nLIGHT is a semiconductor and fiber laser company primarily serving aerospace/defense (directed energy weapons) and industrial markets, with an emerging but unproven angle on AI data center photonics. While the stock surged on NVIDIA's $4B optical industry bet and the broader copper-to-photonics narrative, nLIGHT's actual data center revenue is minimal today -- this is a defense company wearing AI clothing. Record FY25 results ($261M, +32%) are driven by defense contracts, not data center demand.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Moderate (2.5/5)**

- **Defense moat**: Strong position in directed energy weapons (70kW-class laser system). Proprietary high-power fiber laser technology with military qualifications.
- **Semiconductor laser platform**: Vertically integrated from epitaxial wafer growth to packaged lasers.
- **AI photonics angle is aspirational**: The market is speculating that nLIGHT's laser expertise could translate to data center photonics (pump lasers, amplifiers for CPO). But there are no disclosed hyperscaler customers or data center design wins.
- **Competition**: In data center photonics, Lumentum and Coherent have decades of head start. nLIGHT would be entering from a position of weakness.

### Catalysts
1. **NVIDIA optical industry investment narrative**: The $4B NVIDIA bet on photonics lifted all laser stocks. If nLIGHT can secure a data center design win, the re-rating could be significant.
2. **Defense spending tailwinds**: Directed energy weapons budgets are growing. Core business is strong.
3. **Gross margin expansion**: From 17% to 30% in one year. If this continues, profitability improves meaningfully.
4. **Potential data center pivot**: Management commentary on copper-to-photonics transition could signal strategic shift.

### Risks
1. **AI narrative without substance**: Stock has surged on the photonics narrative, but actual data center revenue is negligible. This is primarily a defense stock.
2. **Expensive valuation**: $3.5B market cap on $261M revenue and $23.5M EBITDA. The AI premium is massive.
3. **Q1 guidance step-down**: $70-76M vs $81.2M Q4 suggests the business is lumpy, not accelerating.
4. **Defense budget cyclicality**: Government contract wins are binary and unpredictable.
5. **Data center competition**: Lumentum, Coherent, and II-VI have established laser platforms for optical communications. nLIGHT would be a late entrant.

### Valuation
At $3.5B market cap on $261M revenue (13.4x sales) and $23.5M EBITDA (149x), nLIGHT is priced as if the AI data center pivot is certain to succeed. If the company remains primarily a defense laser company, fair value is likely $1-1.5B (4-6x sales). The AI optionality is real but unpriced-for in terms of probability. This is a speculation, not an investment.

### Verdict: AVOID
nLIGHT has interesting technology and a strong defense franchise, but the AI photonics angle is speculative sentiment, not commercial reality. The stock has been swept up in the broader photonics mania without demonstrating data center design wins, hyperscaler relationships, or a credible product roadmap for optical communications. Wait for concrete evidence of data center revenue before revisiting. For AI photonics exposure, COHR/LITE/FN offer proven demand.

**Final Score: 49/100**
