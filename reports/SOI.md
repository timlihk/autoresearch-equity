# SOI.PA - Soitec SA
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 65/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Soitec is the hidden monopoly of silicon photonics -- the only volume-approved supplier of photonics-grade SOI (silicon-on-insulator) wafers for Tower Semiconductor, GlobalFoundries, and TSMC, holding 70% of the overall SOI wafer market via its proprietary Smart Cut technology. As every silicon photonics transceiver starts with a Soitec wafer, the company sits at the absolute foundation of the optical interconnect stack. The catch: overall revenue is declining (-9% FY25) due to weak smartphone/mobile SOI demand, masking the fast-growing photonics-SOI segment (~€100M, growing double-digits).

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Very Strong (4.5/5)**

Soitec's moat may be the deepest in the entire AI photonics value chain:
- **Smart Cut monopoly**: Soitec's proprietary Smart Cut technology is the industry standard for producing SOI wafers. This IP-based moat has been developed over 20+ years and is extremely difficult to replicate.
- **Only volume photonics-SOI supplier**: Tower Semiconductor, GlobalFoundries, and TSMC -- the three major silicon photonics foundries -- have all approved Soitec as their sole volume supplier. This means every silicon photonics chip from Broadcom, Intel, Cisco, and others starts with a Soitec wafer.
- **70% SOI market share**: Near-monopoly with Shin-Etsu and GlobalWafers as distant competitors.
- **Foundry expansion = Soitec growth**: Tower is investing $900M+ to 5x SiPh capacity by end 2026. Every new Tower SiPh wafer start = a Soitec substrate order. This is a direct, mechanical demand linkage.
- **300mm fab capacity**: Fabs in Singapore and Bernin (France) are expanding to meet demand.
- **Weakness**: Mobile/smartphone SOI (largest segment) is cyclically weak, dragging overall revenue. Photonics is still only ~€100M of €891M total.

### Catalysts
1. **Tower Semiconductor 5x SiPh capacity expansion**: Direct demand driver for photonics-SOI wafers. Tower's investment maps 1:1 to Soitec substrate orders.
2. **Silicon photonics adoption in 1.6T transceivers**: Coherent, Broadcom, and Intel all use silicon photonics architectures that require SOI wafers.
3. **CPO (co-packaged optics) transition**: CPO architectures use silicon photonics die, driving more SOI wafer demand per data center switch.
4. **Photonics-SOI revenue reaching €100M+**: Crossing this threshold makes the photonics story material to overall financials.
5. **Mobile SOI recovery**: Smartphone cycle recovery would lift the base business, freeing valuation to reflect photonics growth.
6. **Medium-term €2B revenue target with 40% EBITDA**: If achieved, implies significant margin expansion and revenue growth.

### Risks
1. **Mobile SOI decline masking photonics growth**: Overall revenue -9% in FY25. Investors focused on headline decline may miss the photonics inflection.
2. **Analyst consensus 30% below current price**: At €41.30 avg target vs €59 stock, analysts are skeptical. The photonics re-rating thesis may not be consensus yet.
3. **Photonics still small**: €100M of €891M (11% of revenue). Will take 2-3 years before photonics meaningfully drives overall growth.
4. **European listing**: SOI.PA trades on Euronext Paris. US investors have limited access via SOITF OTC. Lower institutional coverage.
5. **Customer concentration in foundries**: Dependent on Tower, GF, and TSMC continuing to buy SOI wafers. If any foundry develops alternative substrates, Soitec loses.
6. **Macro exposure**: Overall business is cyclical (smartphones, automotive, IoT). A macro downturn could further pressure non-photonics segments.

### Valuation
At ~€2.4B market cap on €891M revenue (2.7x sales), Soitec is the cheapest name in the entire AI photonics universe. The 70% SOI market share and monopoly in photonics-SOI wafers is extraordinary moat quality at a bargain valuation. The issue is that the market is pricing in the mobile SOI headwinds, not the photonics-SOI tailwinds. If photonics-SOI reaches €200M+ (plausible within 2-3 years as Tower/GF expand), and mobile SOI stabilizes, Soitec could see €1.1-1.2B revenue with 35%+ EBITDA margins -- implying 2-3x upside from current levels. The risk is that mobile weakness persists and photonics growth is slower than hoped.

### Verdict: HOLD
Soitec is one of the most interesting under-discovered names in this research -- a monopoly substrate supplier at the foundation of silicon photonics, trading at 2.7x sales while the rest of AI photonics trades at 5-25x. The Smart Cut moat is world-class and the Tower Semiconductor capacity expansion provides a direct demand catalyst. The headwind is that 89% of revenue is non-photonics and declining. Would upgrade to BUY on: (1) evidence of mobile SOI stabilization, (2) photonics-SOI exceeding €150M run rate, or (3) stock pullback to €45-50 range. For patient investors with a 2-3 year horizon and Euronext access, this is the best value in AI photonics.
