### Key Revenue Drivers
1. **AI back-end networking**: 800G switches connecting GPU clusters. AI revenue doubling to $3.25B in FY26.
2. **Campus/enterprise**: Growing campus switching (CX series). Diversifying beyond hyperscale.
3. **Cloud titan upgrades**: Microsoft, Meta, and others upgrading to 400G/800G across their entire network fleet.
4. **Software and services**: CloudVision network management platform. Recurring revenue growing.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Exceptional (5/5)**

Arista has the deepest moat in AI networking:
- **EOS operating system**: Arista's Extensible Operating System is the industry standard for programmable, cloud-grade networking. Millions of lines of code, 20+ years of development. Switching costs are enormous.
- **40%+ 800G market share**: In the fastest-growing segment, Arista leads. The R4 Series 800G platform reduces AI job completion time by 44%.
- **Cloud titan relationships**: Architectural standard-bearer for Microsoft, Meta, and others. These relationships span 10+ years and involve deep co-engineering.
- **Gross margin durability**: 64% gross margin on networking hardware is extraordinary -- reflects software-like value capture on a hardware product.
- **$10.7B net cash**: Can fund any R&D, M&A, or capital return program without external financing.
- **Network effect**: As EOS becomes the standard, the ecosystem (tools, integrations, talent) grows, reinforcing the moat.

### Supply Chain Relevance (Downstream Demand Signal)
Arista is the most important **downstream demand signal** for all photonics companies:
- **Transceivers**: Arista switches consume optical transceivers from Innolight (300308.SZ), Coherent (COHR), Lumentum (LITE), AAOI
- **AEC cables**: Credo (CRDO) AEC cables connect between Arista switch ports
- **Retimers**: Credo (CRDO) retimers used in Arista line cards
- **Switching ASICs**: Broadcom Tomahawk 5/6 (manufactured on Tower Semi SiPh platform for optical I/O)
- **Test equipment**: VIAV instruments test Arista's deployed networks
- When Arista guides AI revenue +100%, it means MORE transceivers, MORE AECs, MORE laser chips from every company upstream.

### Catalysts
1. **AI revenue doubling to $3.25B**: Strongest forward-looking AI demand signal in the industry.

3. **TAM expansion $60B → $105B**: Management sees massive market expansion from AI networking demand.

5. **$10.7B cash optionality**: Buybacks, M&A, or special dividends all possible.

### Risks
1. **Customer concentration**: Microsoft + Meta = ~40% of revenue. Capex cycles at these two companies drive Arista's results.
2. **Valuation premium**: 51x trailing P/E and 20x sales. A growth slowdown triggers multiple compression.
3. **Broadcom custom silicon competition**: Hyperscalers (Google, Amazon) increasingly design custom switches, potentially disintermediating Arista.
4. **Cisco competitive response**: Cisco is investing heavily in AI networking. While behind today, Cisco has enterprise reach Arista lacks.
5. **Growth deceleration risk**: Revenue growth slowing from 29% to 25% guided. Market expects acceleration, not deceleration.

### Valuation
At $178B market cap, Arista trades at ~40x FY26E earnings ($4.5B) and ~32x FY27E earnings ($5.5B). For a company with 64% gross margins, 43% operating margins, $4.25B FCF, and 25%+ revenue growth, this is a reasonable premium. The net cash ($10.7B, ~6% of market cap) provides downside support.
