# 6503.T - Mitsubishi Electric
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 55/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Mitsubishi Electric holds a remarkable ~50% global market share in EML (electro-absorption modulated laser) chips -- the critical laser component inside 800G and 1.6T optical transceivers. The company is tripling optical semiconductor production by FY2028 to meet AI-driven demand. However, like Sumitomo Electric, Mitsubishi is a ¥5.5T ($37B) conglomerate where optical devices are a small fraction of total revenue, limiting the photonics re-rating potential.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Strong in EML (4/5), but diluted by conglomerate**
- 50% global EML chip share is extraordinary. Mass production of 200Gbps single-chip EMLs for 1.6T transceivers.
- But Lumentum leads in the highest-end 200G/lane EMLs shipping at volume. Mitsubishi's 50% likely includes broader EML categories including lower-speed.
- Tripling production capacity is bullish signal but won't materially change Mitsubishi's overall financials.

### Supply Chain Relevance
- **Key supplier to**: Innolight (300308.SZ), transceiver ODMs, Coherent module division
- **Competes with**: Lumentum (LITE) in high-end EMLs, Broadcom, Sumitomo Electric
- **Upstream**: InP substrates from AXT, Sumitomo, JX Metals

### Verdict: HOLD
Same thesis as Sumitomo Electric (5802.T) -- incredible optical technology buried in a massive conglomerate. The ~50% EML share and 3x capacity expansion are genuine AI photonics catalysts, but they're immaterial to a ¥5.5T revenue base. For pure EML laser exposure, Lumentum (LITE) is the better play. For Japan-listed conglomerate optionality with AI photonics upside, Mitsubishi is marginally more interesting than Sumitomo due to the higher EML share.

**Final Score: 55/100**
