# 005930.KS - Samsung Electronics
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 71/100
## Memory Chokepoint Tier: Oligopoly with Moat

### Thesis
Samsung is still one of the three indispensable global memory suppliers, and the broad memory supercycle should lift its DRAM, HBM4, and enterprise SSD business materially. The problem is not scale. It is execution. Samsung's memory franchise is enormous, but investors still need proof that it can translate that scale into clear HBM leadership rather than just participating in a favorable market.

### Value Chain Position
Samsung sits across almost the entire memory stack:
- HBM and conventional DRAM
- NAND and enterprise SSD
- memory base-die plus advanced packaging integration through its logic and foundry assets

That breadth gives it optionality, but also makes the stock less pure than Micron or SK hynix.

### Supply Chain Map
**Upstream:** Samsung controls more of its own ecosystem than peers, from advanced nodes to packaging and foundry integration.

**Downstream:** hyperscalers, server OEMs, mobile OEMs, storage customers, and Samsung's own device businesses.

**Competitors:** SK hynix and Micron. Samsung's real battle is not existence; it is closing the execution gap in HBM.

**Displacement risk:** continued HBM share lag or too much investor focus on weak non-memory segments.

### Moat Analysis
Samsung's moat is scale, process depth, and balance sheet:
- it remains one of the few companies that can fund every part of the memory roadmap
- management said the AI boom should keep industry conditions favorable in 2026
- the company plans timely HBM4 shipments and broader AI-memory mix through DDR5, SOCAMM2, GDDR7, and SSDs

But this moat is partially masked by execution doubts and by the stock's conglomerate discount.

### Catalysts
1. HBM4 mass-product deliveries are planned, including the faster 11.7Gbps SKU.
2. AI server demand should also support high-density DDR5 and enterprise SSD.
3. Samsung's foundry plus memory integration offers upside if custom AI-memory packaging becomes more important.
4. The stock still has room to rerate if HBM perception improves.

### Risks
1. Samsung is not the first-call HBM name today; SK hynix owns that position.
2. Broader business segments can dilute the impact of a great memory cycle.
3. The market may continue to assign a discount until HBM execution is clearly proven.
4. Large capex and broad corporate complexity can slow responsiveness versus more focused peers.

### Verdict: HOLD
Samsung benefits from the memory supercycle, but the stock is a lower-conviction way to express the thesis than SK hynix, Micron, or the sharper bottlenecks in test and bonding.

### Price Target: KRW 220,000
This target assumes stronger HBM execution and modest multiple expansion, not a full re-rating to the premium assigned to the current memory leaders.
