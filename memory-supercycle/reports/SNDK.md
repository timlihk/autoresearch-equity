# SNDK - Sandisk
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 77/100
## Memory Chokepoint Tier: Qualified Supplier with Leverage

### Thesis
Sandisk is a newly separated flash pure-play that is more levered to AI storage demand than many investors appreciate. Management has said demand for its NAND products is outpacing supply and that AI is creating a strong tailwind for its high-capacity, power-efficient SSDs. This is not an HBM choke point, but it is a credible way to own the storage side of the memory supercycle.

### Value Chain Position
Sandisk sits in the NAND and SSD layer:
- flash-memory economics through the Kioxia joint venture
- enterprise and data-center SSDs
- future optionality in High Bandwidth Flash (HBF) for AI inference

As inference scales, fast and power-efficient storage becomes more important alongside DRAM.

### Supply Chain Map
**Upstream:** Kioxia joint-venture wafer supply, BiCS node transitions, controller ecosystem, packaging, and enterprise qualification.

**Downstream:** hyperscalers, storage OEMs, enterprise customers, edge devices, and consumers.

**Competitors:** Kioxia, Samsung, Micron, SK hynix / Solidigm, and other flash suppliers.

**Displacement risk:** NAND pricing normalizes too quickly, or enterprise SSD qualification cadence slips.

### Moat Analysis
Sandisk's moat is narrower than HBM suppliers, but stronger than a commodity label suggests:
- in its fiscal Q1 2026 presentation, management said demand for NAND products continued to outpace supply
- data-center revenue grew 26% sequentially in that quarter
- management highlighted accelerating enterprise SSD deployments in fiscal Q2 2026
- Sandisk's HBF concept is explicitly aimed at augmenting HBM for AI inference workloads, with a claimed path to much higher capacity at similar cost

That gives the stock both near-term flash recovery and longer-term AI-memory optionality.

### Catalysts
1. Demand is outpacing supply and pricing remains favorable.
2. Enterprise SSD deployments are accelerating.
3. Balance-sheet improvement after separation gives management more flexibility.
4. HBF could become a differentiated AI-inference memory/storage product if commercialization progresses.

### Risks
1. The stock has rerated hard and expectations are now much higher.
2. Flash remains cyclical and can mean-revert faster than HBM.
3. The Kioxia JV creates dependency and overlap with another major supplier.
4. HBF remains optionality, not current earnings power.

### Verdict: BUY
Sandisk is not the purest choke point in the project, but it is one of the best undercovered ways to own AI-driven storage demand with improving financials and real supply discipline.

### Price Target: $850
That target assumes enterprise SSD demand remains strong, margins keep improving, and investors continue to value Sandisk as an AI-storage beneficiary rather than just a cyclical flash name.
