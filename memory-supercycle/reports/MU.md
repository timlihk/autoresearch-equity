# MU - Micron Technology
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 86/100
## Memory Chokepoint Tier: Oligopoly with Moat

### Thesis
Micron is the cleanest U.S.-listed way to own the memory supercycle. It now has simultaneous exposure to HBM4, server DRAM, and enterprise SSDs just as AI drives both DRAM and NAND supply tightness beyond calendar 2026. The stock is no longer a simple commodity-memory rebound trade; it is a qualified-supply story with structurally better mix and unusually strong pricing power.

### Value Chain Position
Micron sits at the fabrication layer of the memory stack and increasingly sells into the highest-value parts of it:
- HBM4 for AI accelerators
- Server DRAM and LP memory for AI systems
- Data-center SSDs for inference, vector databases, and capacity tiers

Micron matters because there are only a few global companies that can qualify and ship these products at scale.

### Supply Chain Map
**Upstream:** wafer equipment vendors, packaging capacity, substrates, and Micron's own node transitions such as 1-gamma DRAM and G9 NAND.

**Downstream:** NVIDIA-linked accelerator platforms, hyperscalers, server OEMs, enterprise storage customers, handset OEMs, and automotive customers.

**Competitors:** SK hynix, Samsung. In HBM the field is especially narrow; in enterprise SSD it is wider, but qualification still matters.

**Displacement risk:** faster-than-expected HBM share loss to SK hynix or Samsung, or a broad AI-capex slowdown.

### Moat Analysis
Micron's moat is not monopoly share. It is the combination of:
- one of only three global scale memory manufacturers
- direct HBM4 qualification with NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform
- record margins from AI-heavy mix
- strong position in data-center SSDs as inference increases storage intensity

Micron said on March 18, 2026 that data-center DRAM and NAND TAM should exceed 50% of industry TAM for the first time in calendar 2026. That is the key point: the best part of the market is becoming the market.

### Catalysts
1. HBM4 volume shipments are already underway.
2. Micron expects both DRAM and NAND supply to remain tight beyond 2026.
3. Data-center NAND revenues more than doubled sequentially in fiscal Q2.
4. Fiscal 2027 capex is set to step up again for HBM- and DRAM-related investment, which confirms management's confidence in demand visibility.

### Risks
1. Memory remains cyclical, and pricing can reverse faster than investors expect.
2. SK hynix is still the stronger HBM franchise, so Micron must prove sustained share and yield execution.
3. Capex is rising sharply, which increases execution risk if demand softens.
4. Geopolitical concentration around Asia-based manufacturing and packaging remains a structural risk.

### Verdict: BUY
Micron has crossed from cyclical recovery into strategic scarcity. At 75% gross margin and with clear HBM4 and AI-SSD traction, it is one of the most direct beneficiaries of the memory supercycle.

### Price Target: $520
That target assumes the market pays a premium multiple for a memory maker with record margins, HBM4 credibility, and supply tightness that now extends beyond the usual one-year visibility window.
