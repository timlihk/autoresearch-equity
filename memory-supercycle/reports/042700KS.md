# 042700.KS - Hanmi Semiconductor
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 83/100
## Memory Chokepoint Tier: Monopoly / Duopoly Chokepoint

### Thesis
Hanmi is the purest picks-and-shovels play inside the HBM bottleneck because its TC bonders are critical to stacking the DRAM dies that make HBM possible. If memory is the supercycle, Hanmi is the equipment bottleneck inside the best part of that cycle. The risk is concentration and competition, but the reward is unusually high leverage to every incremental HBM capacity plan.

### Value Chain Position
Hanmi does not make memory. It makes one of the key tools required to turn memory dies into HBM stacks:
- thermal-compression bonders for HBM production
- newer TC Bonder 4 systems aimed at HBM4
- roadmap products for later HBM generations

That means Hanmi benefits when customers expand HBM capacity, even before the finished memory ships.

### Supply Chain Map
**Upstream:** precision components, software, assembly, inspection, and Hanmi's own vertically integrated manufacturing base.

**Downstream:** SK hynix, Micron, and potentially other memory makers that need HBM bond capacity.

**Competitors:** Hanwha Semitech and other emerging alternatives. Competition is real, but still narrow.

**Displacement risk:** customer insourcing, faster-than-expected share loss, or a slowdown in HBM capacity additions.

### Moat Analysis
Hanmi's moat is process-position, not size:
- its TC bonders are built for the exact HBM stacking step that customers cannot skip
- Korea press reports indicate Hanmi was effectively the sole tool provider for SK hynix's 12-layer HBM3E process before the customer diversified supply
- the company filed more than 120 HBM-equipment patents according to company-linked reporting
- its manufacturing is vertically integrated enough to produce very high operating margins for an equipment company

### Catalysts
1. SK hynix ordered additional TC bonders in January 2026 for HBM4-related production.
2. Hanmi's TC Bonder 4 is aimed at sixth-generation HBM4 production.
3. New wide-TC bonder products should extend the roadmap toward later HBM generations.
4. Overseas customers already represented about 90% of expected 1Q25 sales, showing the story is broadening beyond one domestic customer.

### Risks
1. Customer concentration remains the biggest risk.
2. Hanwha and others are trying to win share in TC bonders.
3. Equipment demand is lumpy even in strong end-markets.
4. The stock is volatile and can correct sharply on order timing headlines.

### Verdict: BUY
Hanmi is not the safest way to own the memory supercycle, but it is one of the most direct. Inside HBM packaging, few listed names offer this level of operating leverage.

### Price Target: KRW 360,000
That target assumes continued HBM tool demand, sustained high margins, and no major disruption to Hanmi's position in TC bonders.
