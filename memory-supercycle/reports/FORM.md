# FORM - FormFactor
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 79/100
## Memory Chokepoint Tier: Oligopoly with Moat

### Thesis
FormFactor is the underfollowed consumables and probe-card play on the memory supercycle. It does not get the attention of HBM makers, but it already ships in volume to all three major HBM manufacturers. As HBM complexity rises, test consumables become more valuable, and FormFactor can benefit even if memory pricing itself becomes choppy.

### Value Chain Position
FormFactor sits in the probe and test-consumables layer:
- DRAM and HBM probe cards
- advanced probe-card components
- adjacent advanced-packaging consumables

This is a bottleneck because wafer sort gets harder as memory speed, density, and thermals increase.

### Supply Chain Map
**Upstream:** high-performance components, substrates, and FormFactor's manufacturing footprint, including its link to FICT.

**Downstream:** all three major HBM manufacturers, broader DRAM customers, and advanced packaging customers.

**Competitors:** a small set of probe-card and test-interface vendors.

**Displacement risk:** customer insourcing, margin pressure from new ramps, or a pause in HBM-capacity additions.

### Moat Analysis
FormFactor's moat is qualification plus consumables intensity:
- management said in July 2025 that it was shipping in volume to all three major HBM manufacturers
- FY2024 HBM revenue grew fourfold
- DRAM probe-card revenue set three straight quarterly records by early 2025
- the FICT transaction improves access to high-performance advanced probe-card components

This is exactly the kind of underfollowed bottleneck that a chokepoint framework should surface.

### Catalysts
1. HBM ramps continue to pull more probe-card demand through the system.
2. The company is already qualified across all three major HBM makers.
3. Any move from HBM3E to HBM4 should raise test complexity further.
4. Investors can discover the name as a cleaner bottleneck than high-beta memory prices.

### Risks
1. Margin pressure can show up during customer ramps and factory transitions.
2. Probe-card revenue is still lumpy quarter to quarter.
3. The company is smaller than the dominant memory makers and can move sharply with sentiment.
4. A pause in HBM ramps would hit the narrative fast.

### Verdict: BUY
FormFactor is the most underappreciated stock in this sub-project. It has real HBM exposure, a qualified position across the full customer set, and far less direct commodity-memory risk than the memory makers themselves.

### Price Target: $120
That target assumes the market starts paying up for a scarce HBM-enablement business rather than treating FormFactor like a generic semi-test supplier.
