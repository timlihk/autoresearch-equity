## The Three Best Ideas — Deep Dive

### #1: NUTRIEN 4.20% 2029 — "The Fertilizer Channel"

**The trade most people won't think of.**

- **Mechanism**: Qatar produces ~6M tons/yr of ammonia and urea. Force majeure declared. One-third of global seaborne fertilizer transits Hormuz. Urea already +25-30% to $591-674/ton
- **Why Nutrien**: World's largest fertilizer producer. Canadian potash + Alberta nitrogen. Feedstock cost: $2-3/mmbtu gas (vs $15+ Asian LNG). Every dollar of urea price increase flows to EBITDA with near-zero cost offset
- **Credit math**: $500M-$1B incremental EBITDA from nitrogen pricing alone. Leverage drops from 1.8x to 1.3-1.5x. Credible upgrade to BBB+
- **Downside**: Farmers must buy fertilizer. Russian/Belarusian sanctions provide price floor. Bond matures 2029 (short duration)
- **Spread tightening**: 15-25bps on upgrade = meaningful price appreciation on a 3yr bond
- **Edge**: Everyone buys "oil stocks" for Hormuz. Fertilizer is the less crowded, more mechanical trade

### #2: CHENIERE CQP 4.50% Oct 2029 — "Structural LNG Gap"

**The multi-year supply deficit play.**

- **Mechanism**: Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG terminal damaged. 12.8 Mtpa offline. 3-5 YEAR repair timeline. This is not a price spike — it's a structural supply hole that US LNG fills
- **Why Cheniere**: Only scaled US LNG exporter with long-term contracts. 95%+ contracted = floor EBITDA. Spot/uncontracted volumes reprice massively higher. Already upgraded to BBB+ in Nov 2025
- **Credit math**: EBITDA from $6.9B to $9-11B. Leverage from 3.5x to 2.2-2.7x. Full notch compression into A- territory
- **Downside**: 95% contracted capacity means floor EBITDA regardless. S&P stable outlook
- **Edge**: Equity has repriced (+45% YTD). Bonds typically lag equity in recognizing positive credit developments

### #3: MPC 5.15% Mar 2030 — "Crack Spread Explosion"

**The refining margin play.**

- **Mechanism**: Brent-WTI spread blown to $12-15/bbl (vs normal $3-5). Diesel crack at $64/bbl (78% of all-time record). US refiners buy cheap domestic WTI, sell refined products at global Brent-linked prices
- **Why MPC**: Largest US refiner by capacity (~3.0M bbl/day). At current cracks, refining EBITDA annualizes $15-20B. Already low leverage (1.29x) compresses below 1.0x
- **Credit math**: Leverage below 1.0x on a BBB-rated credit = strong case for upgrade to BBB+ or A-. 40-60bps spread tightening potential
- **Downside**: Even at normalized cracks ($15-20/bbl), MPC generates solid cash flow. US refining capacity is structurally tight
- **Edge**: Biggest absolute spread tightening potential of any name in the group
