# Hormuz Disruption — Value Chain & Chokepoint Map

## The Chokepoint: Strait of Hormuz

```
Width: 21 nautical miles (narrowest navigable channel: 2 miles each direction)
Daily oil transit: ~20-21M bbl/day (~20% of global supply)
Daily LNG transit: ~14B cf/day (Qatar = world's #1 LNG exporter)
Countries dependent on Hormuz for export: Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Kuwait, UAE, Qatar, Iran, Bahrain
```

## Bypass Capacity (limited)

| Route | Capacity | Status |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Saudi East-West Pipeline (Petroline) | 5M bbl/day to Red Sea | Operational but only ~50% of Saudi exports |
| Abu Dhabi ADCOP Pipeline to Fujairah | 1.5M bbl/day | Operational, bypasses Hormuz |
| Iraq-Turkey Pipeline (Kirkuk-Ceyhan) | 1.6M bbl/day | Intermittent, security risk |
| **Total bypass** | **~8M bbl/day** | **Leaves ~12-13M bbl/day stranded** |

### Layer 3: Refiners (crack spread explosion)

Key insight: US refiners buy WTI crude (landlocked, discounted) and sell refined products (gasoline, diesel, jet fuel) at global prices. If Brent spikes to $150 but WTI only goes to $120, the WTI-Brent spread + crack spread = massive margin expansion.

### Layer 4: Defense (escalation = budget)

Hormuz conflict → US/allied military response → accelerated procurement. Defense primes have IG-rated debt AND multi-year backlogs that insulate from demand cycles.

## Key Risks to the Thesis

1. **Disruption may be short-lived** (days/weeks). Bonds may not move fast enough to capture
2. **Global recession risk** from oil shock could hurt ALL credits, even beneficiaries
3. **US SPR release** (currently ~400M barrels) buys ~40 days of lost Hormuz supply
4. **Saudi bypass capacity** (5M bbl/day East-West pipeline) partially mitigates
5. **Iran may not actually close Hormuz** — it's mutual assured economic destruction (Iran exports through Hormuz too)
6. **Basis risk**: even "beneficiary" bonds may sell off in a broad risk-off event before the credit improvement is recognized
